Year 10 English · Animal Farm

Through the Lens

Read Animal Farm in four different ways

A lens is a way of looking at a story. Different lenses help you see different things in the same words.

In this game you will use four lenses, one at a time. You read a piece of Animal Farm, then click any line that the lens helps you notice. You can switch lenses any time.

Pick a lens to start with. (You will use all four.)

No timer. No score. Take as long as you need.

How it works

  1. Pick a lens. Each lens helps you notice different things in the same words.
  2. Click a line in the story that the lens helps you see. A coloured underline appears.
  3. Click a different lens. Click another line. You can mark the same line under more than one lens -- the lens letter (H, F, C, R) will appear before it.
  4. Sometimes a small box pops up asking you to write one or two sentences. That helps you remember what you noticed.
  5. Click "Develop the photograph" when you've tried each lens. You'll see what an expert noticed too.

You cannot get anything wrong. Marking a line that an expert didn't mark is fine -- you might be seeing something they missed. The Reflection screen shows everyone's marks.

Keyboard: Tab to move between buttons. Enter or Space to choose. Esc to pause.
